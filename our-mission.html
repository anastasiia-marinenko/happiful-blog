About Us

Here at Happiful, our mission is to create a healthier, happier and more sustainable society and to challenge the stigma around mental health. And, we’re sticklers for practising what we publish

Background

Happiful Magazine was created by two sisters, Emma and Aimi, driven by their shared passion for mental health and wellbeing. In 2005, they launched Counselling Directory, a platform designed to connect people with professional therapists in their local area.

Over the years, their vision expanded, and they introduced four additional wellness directories focusing on life coaching, hypnotherapy, nutrition, and complementary therapies. Happiful Magazine became the next step in their mission—to spread awareness that help and support are always within reach.

Our goal is to share stories, experiences, and resources that support the wellbeing of everyone in our community. At the heart of everything we do are our core values. We believe that if we’re not passionate about our work, our readers won’t be either.

Our Team

The Happiful team is based in Surrey, UK, and we are currently at around 40 team members. Together we work on the wellbeing directories and magazine. From our award-winning customer service team to our incredible team of developers, we are all passionate about the four pillars of our mission.

The Happiful team at Leeds Castle in July 2024

Our mission

It’s important to us that we strive to achieve our mission in everything we do, while maintaining our family values. The Happiful mission consists of four statements:

  1. To raise awareness of mental health – because we all have it. We are passionate about informing and supporting people who are looking for help.
  2. To bring people together – whether it’s connecting people to professional help, or building friendships in the office. Together we are dedicated to making a difference.
  3. To end the stigma of mental health. Mental illness and poor mental health are not things to be ashamed about, we are proud to lead the way in allowing people to publish their stories with their own voice.
  4. To have a positive impact on the world around us – through looking after our staff, our readers, our society and the planet.
