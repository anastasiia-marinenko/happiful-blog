How Designing Our Homes Around Nature Could Be the Future

By Katie Scott, updated on Jan 9, 2025

Exploring why the art of consciously bringing the outside world into our homes is so good for our souls

We yearn for a connection with nature. It’s hard-wired into us and even has a name: biophilia. Meaning a love of life or living things, the term was coined by social psychologist Erich Fromm in 1964, before becoming popularised by E. O. Wilson, a biologist and Professor Emeritus at Harvard.

However, designers and architects have only recently begun to fully appreciate the benefits of integrating natural elements into living spaces. Homes designed with nature in mind can improve mental well-being, reduce stress, and even enhance cognitive function.

Consider elements like natural lighting, indoor plants, and water features. These not only add to the aesthetic but also positively impact the inhabitants' mental and physical health.

Why It Matters

Research has shown that incorporating nature into interior spaces can significantly lower cortisol levels, the hormone associated with stress. Exposure to natural elements is linked to improved focus and a heightened sense of well-being.

How to Bring Nature Home

Here are some tips for integrating nature into your home:

  • Use natural materials like wood and stone in furniture and decor.
  • Maximize natural light by using sheer curtains or skylights.
  • Incorporate greenery with indoor plants that thrive in your environment.
  • Create outdoor-like spaces indoors, such as a sunroom or a green wall.

For more inspiration, consider consulting with a biophilic design specialist or exploring online resources that focus on nature-integrated architecture.
