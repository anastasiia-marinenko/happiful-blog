Savour Memories Without Building Clutter (With Tips From A Professional Declutterer)

By Caroline Butterwick, updated on Oct 12, 2024

Investigating the power of possessions to evoke emotions, and transport us through time – and how we can treasure them, without them taking over our homes

After a three-month renovation, I’m left with boxes filled with objects I can’t seem to let go of. These items evoke memories – small fingers presenting paper maché creations, or shells collected on a beach. Objects hold power, serving as reminders of meaningful moments and links to the past.

Productivity coach Juliet Landau-Pope explains: “Material things tie us to the past, triggering memories of both individual and collective experiences.” Similarly, Chris McCarroll, author of *Repair Shop of Memory*, describes objects as “vibrant materials” that scaffold our memories. Restoring or displaying them can reanimate cherished moments.

When Memories Weigh Us Down

Not all memories are joyful. McCarroll notes that some items can evoke painful associations, but even these can offer lessons. Declutterer Siân Pelleschi highlights a difference between items that make us feel bad and those that prompt “happy sadness” – reminders of loved ones we’ve lost but cherish.

When an object becomes too emotionally heavy, experts recommend removing it from your space. McCarroll suggests shaping your environment to minimize triggers and focus on positive associations. Landau-Pope advises creating memory boxes or taking photos to preserve memories without physical clutter.

Balancing Nostalgia and Space

Striking a balance is key. “Our homes shouldn’t be museums,” Pelleschi says. Too many items can overwhelm, making it difficult to function. Curating and rotating displays allows you to enjoy your treasures while maintaining a practical, relaxing space.

Tips for Managing Memorabilia

  • Keep items that evoke positive memories and joy.
  • Display small groups of objects to avoid overcrowding and create focus.
  • Create memory boxes for items not in use but still cherished.
  • Rotate displays seasonally or by theme to keep your space fresh.
  • Label storage boxes to ensure you can easily access items when needed.

Ultimately, our homes should honor the past, inspire the future, and support living in the present. By carefully curating our belongings, we create a space that reflects our stories while leaving room for new memories.
